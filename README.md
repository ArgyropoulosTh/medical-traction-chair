# Medical Traction Chair

Therapeutic medical device designed to apply controlled lumbar traction for patients with lower back pain, focusing on biomechanical safety, structural integrity, and clinical usability.

**Status:** Completed
**Role:** BioMechanical Design & Development Engineer
**Institution:** TU Delft — Medical Device Prototyping Course
**Duration:** 6 months
**Domain:** Medical Device • Biomechanics • Therapeutic Systems

---

##  Overview

This project involved the design and development of a therapeutic chair capable of applying controlled lumbar traction to patients suffering from lower back pain. The system was developed through a multidisciplinary collaboration to ensure clinical relevance, safety, and functional effectiveness.

The work combined biomechanical design, structural validation, and system-level integration, resulting in a functional prototype evaluated within an academic medical device development framework.

---

## Project Context & Recognition

Developed during the **Medical Device Prototyping** course at TU Delft within a multidisciplinary team.

**Team composition:**

* Mechanical Engineer (my role)
